Inside Bar Block: bar-2 is fully inside bar-3; bars 0 and 3 each form their own FVG with bar-2 (double FVG condition). Block zone = bar-1 high / bar-1 low. The zone is the candle directly before the confirming bar.

Walk-Forward Analysis — rolling train/test windows with parameter re-optimization

Splits the active dataset into rolling train/test windows, re-optimizes parameters on each train window, and evaluates the chosen parameters on the next never-seen test window. The stitched out-of-sample equity curve is the most honest single picture of expected live performance. Walk-Forward Efficiency (WFE) = OOS total return ÷ in-sample total return; values above ~0.5 mean the optimization is finding genuine edge, below ~0.3 mean the optimizer is curve-fitting noise.

Resampled the trade log with replacement 1000 times. The narrower the CI, the more confident you can be in the point estimate. CI widths above ~50% of the point estimate suggest the sample is too small for confident inference.

Probabilistic Sharpe Ratio — Bailey & Lopez de Prado, 2012

PSR = P(true Sharpe ≥ 0). Requires at least ~30 trades and accounts for skew/kurtosis of per-trade returns. PSR > 95% means you can claim the strategy has a positive Sharpe with conventional academic confidence; 50–95% means likely positive but not statistically certain; < 50% means the data is consistent with a zero-Sharpe strategy.

Robustness ladder — how does the edge degrade with friction?

Re-runs the strategy with a fill buffer of 0, 0.25, 0.5, 1, and 2 ticks (price must trade through entry by N ticks before the order fills). Mirrors the Python fill-realism test. A strategy whose edge collapses past 1 tick is a fill-mechanic artifact; one that holds at 2 ticks is real.
